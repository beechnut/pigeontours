## What to expect on a tour

When you sign up, we'll send you an email with tour-specific details about accessibility, parking, and what to&nbsp;bring.

Here's some general information about what you can expect on a typical&nbsp;tour.


### Accessibility

Tours involve about a mile of movement. We choose routes that are easy to travel, but unexpected obstacles may&nbsp;occur.

If you have specific mobility needs or questions, please get in touch and we'll work to meet your&nbsp;needs.


### Transportation

Our tours are held in locations walkable from public&nbsp;transportation.

In Philly, tours are located near 2-hour free street parking or paid parking, and the tours are 90 minutes so you have some&nbsp;buffer.

When you register, location-specific transportation and parking advice will be sent to&nbsp;you.


### What to bring

Pigeon tours are rain-or-shine &mdash; just like&nbsp;pigeons &mdash; so make sure to be prepared.

<div class="list-component style-1" markdown="1">
- Weather-appropriate clothes and footwear
- Sun protection, including sunscreen, sunglasses, and maybe a hat.
- Water
- Cash (or Venmo) if you'd like to tip your guide
- Your binoculars, if you have a pair you like
</div>

If you don't have your own binoculars, no worries &mdash; you can borrow one of our loaner&nbsp;pairs!
