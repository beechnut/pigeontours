# Global Pigeon Tour

Did you go on a Philly Pigeon Tour and say, "I could use ten more days of that?"

Are you interested in learning about pigeons, in the places where pigeon history happened?

The **Global Pigeon Tour** will be a journey across the U.S. and Europe, to visit pigeon history landmarks and meet with people who know pigeons.

We are currently **assessing interest** in this tour. It is not scheduled or finalized.

If you are interested &mdash; or at least curious &mdash; please click the button below. You'll be the first to be notified when the tour registration opens, and your (optional) input on a few questions will help us craft a great tour!

### Itinerary (tentative)

<div id="list03" class="list-component instance-3 style-1" markdown="1">
- Start Bryant Park in New York City, where the term "rats with wings" was coined. Learn about how and why pigeons got their undeserved bad reputation.
- If we're lucky, we might get to meet with a person of note in the pigeon world &mdash; such as a pigeon researcher, pigeon sociologist, or pigeon science communicator.
- Fly to Northern Scotland, to visit the Northern Hebrides, cliff-covered islands with large populations of rock doves (a.k.a. pigeons).
- Take a train to London or Wales to meet with the U.K. National Pigeon Advocacy. Get an inside look at their work building a volunteer-run pigeon coop.
- Depending on when this tour runs, the tour might also include a pass to the U.K. National Pigeon Advocacy Conference.
- Optional excursion: Take a bird and landscape photography class with a renowned nature photographer
- Travel to one more city, possibly Berlin or Venice, to see landmark sites in pigeon history, meet with pigeon advocates, and learn more pigeon science.
</div>
